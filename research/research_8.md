## Chosen Niche: Online Education Platform

* **Byju's**:

	+ Valuation: $22 billion (Source: Bloomberg, 2022)
	+ Average monthly subscription fee: ₹1,400 (~$18 USD) (Source: Byju's official website)
* **Unacademy**:
	+ Founded in 2010
	+ Valuation: $3.4 billion (Source: Entrackr, 2022)
	+ Average monthly subscription fee: ₹999 (~$13 USD) (Source: Unacademy official website)
* **Coursera**:
	+ Founded in 2012
	+ Valuation: $4.5 billion (Source: Entrackr, 2022)
	+ Average monthly subscription fee: ₹12,000 - ₹30,000 (~$155 - $390 USD) (Source: Coursera official website)
* **edX**:
	+ Founded in 2012
	+ Valuation: $1 billion (Source: Entrackr, 2022)
	+ Average monthly subscription fee: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000 (~$130 - $320 USD) (Source: edX official website)
* **Udemy**:
	+ Founded in 2010
	+ Valuation: $3.3 billion (Source: Entrackr, 2022)
	+ Average monthly subscription fee: ₹2,000 - ₹5,000 (~$25 - $65 USD) (Source: Udemy official website)
* **Great Learning**:

### Pricing Strategies in India

Here are the pricing strategies adopted by the top 10 competitors in the online education platform niche in India:

* **Tiered pricing**: Byju's, Unacademy, Coursera, and edX offer tiered pricing, where users can choose from different plans based on their needs and budgets.
* **Freemium model**: Khan Academy offers a freemium model, where users can access most of the content for free, while premium features are available for a subscription fee.
* **Dynamic pricing**: UpGrad and Great Learning use dynamic pricing, where the subscription fee varies based on the user's location, education level, and other factors.
* **Discounts and promotions**: Most of the competitors offer discounts and promotions to attract new users and retain existing ones.
* **Free trials**: Byju's, Unacademy, and Vedantu offer free trials to allow users to experience the platform before committing to a paid subscription.

### Market Trends and Opportunities

Here are some market trends and opportunities in the online education platform niche in India:

* **Growing demand for online education**: The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the demand for online education in India, creating a growing market for online education platforms.
* **Increasing competition**: The market is becoming increasingly competitive, with new entrants and existing players offering innovative features and pricing strategies to attract users.
* **Need for personalized learning**: There is a growing need for personalized learning experiences, which online education platforms can offer through AI-powered adaptive technology.
* **Opportunities for niche targeting**: Online education platforms can target specific niches, such as professional courses, language learning, and test preparation, to differentiate themselves from competitors.
